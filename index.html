Take Back Control
What you
can actually do.
You can't stop all of it. But you can meaningfully shrink how much leaves your device. Start here.
```
1
Switch your browser starts here
Your browser collects data before you visit any site. Chrome gathers 20 data types — including your payment details and contact list. Brave collects 3. Firefox is open source and non-profit. This one switch cuts your exposure more than anything else.
HIGHEST IMPACT
2
Switch your search engine
Every Google search builds your profile. DuckDuckGo and Brave Search don't log who you are.
EASY WIN
3
Turn off ad personalisation
Chrome: Settings → Privacy → Ad privacy → off. iPhone: Settings → Privacy → Apple Advertising → off. Takes 60 seconds.
EASY WIN
4
Audit your app permissions
Most apps request more than they need. iPhone: Settings → Privacy. Android: Settings → Apps → Permissions. Revoke anything that doesn't make sense.
MEDIUM EFFORT
5
Stop trusting incognito mode
Incognito hides history from others on your device. Your ISP, Google, and every tracker can still see you. It was never designed to hide you from them.
JUST KNOW THIS
```
About
"I was asking an AI to help me build a privacy tool — and realised I should probably add AI assistants to the list of things tracking you."
The Irony

LookingIn was built with an AI that logs every conversation. That's not a secret — and it's kind of the point. If even the people building privacy tools are using systems that track them, the problem is bigger than most people realise.

The Philosophy

Impact drives the story. If you don't know who is tracking you, you'd never ask why — and you'd never ask how to stop it. So we start with the number. Everything else follows.

Our Commitments
✓Zero network requests — nothing leaves your device
✓No analytics, no ads, no trackers — ever
✓Every line of code is readable — open source
✓No account, no login, no server
✓If we can't stop something, we say so honestly
